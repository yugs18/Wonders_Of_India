Harmandir Sahib, also known as the Golden Temple and Darbar Sahib, is the holiest shrine of Sikhism, located in the holy city of Amritsar, Punjab, India.

  • Holiest shrine of Sikhism: Revered by Sikhs worldwide, it attracts millions of pilgrims every year.
  • Location: Amritsar, Punjab, India.
  • Architecture: A two-story structure built on a square platform in the center of the Sarovar. The upper floor is gilded with approximately 750 kg of gold leaf.

    • Significance:

  • Spiritual center: Houses the Guru Granth Sahib, the Sikh holy scripture.
  • Symbol of inclusivity: Four entrances on all sides welcome people from all faiths and backgrounds.
  • Embodiment of service: The Langar provides free meals to all visitors, regardless of their religion or social status.

    • Other notable features:

  • Akal Takht: The Sikh seat of temporal authority, located opposite the Golden Temple.
  • Jubi Tree: A 450-year-old tree believed to possess special powers.

    • Harmandir Sahib is not just a stunning architectural marvel; it's a beacon of spirituality, inclusivity, and selfless service, offering a profound pilgrimage experience for people of all walks of life.
