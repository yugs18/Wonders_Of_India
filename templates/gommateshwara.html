Gommateshwara

{% endblock %} {% block body %}

Gomateshwara is a monumental statue of Bahubali, a revered figure in Jainism, located in Shravanabelagola, Karnataka, India.

  • Monolithic marvel: Carved from a single block of granite, the statue stands at a majestic 57 feet (17 meters) tall, making it one of the world's tallest monolithic sculptures.
  • Symbol of Jain principles: Gomateshwara embodies the Jain values of ahimsa (non-violence), sanvega (self-restraint), and tyaga (renunciation). His posture, standing still with open eyes for 12 years, represents deep meditation and spiritual liberation.
  • Historical significance: Built around 983 CE by the Western Ganga dynasty, Gomateshwara stands as a testament to the artistic and spiritual achievements of ancient India.
  • Religious pilgrimage: The statue is a major pilgrimage site for Jains, who gather here every 12 years for the Mahamastakabhisheka ceremony, an elaborate ritual anointing of the statue with sacred offerings.
  • Cultural icon: Gomateshwara has transcended religious significance to become a symbol of Karnataka's rich cultural heritage, attracting tourists and art enthusiasts from around the world.
