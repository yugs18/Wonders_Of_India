Hampi

{% endblock %} {% block body %}

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Karnataka, India, is a treasure trove of ancient ruins and rich history.

    A bygone empire's grandeur:

  • Hampi was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, a powerful Hindu kingdom that thrived from the 14th to the 16th centuries.
  • Imagine a bustling metropolis, adorned with ornate temples, towering palaces, and bustling markets. Sadly, it was ransacked in 1565, leaving behind a mesmerizing yet melancholic landscape of ruins.

    • Architectural marvels:

  • Step into the Virupaksha Temple, an active Hindu temple that pulsates with vibrant energy.
  • Marvel at the intricate carvings adorning the Stone Chariot, a monolithic granite structure resembling a chariot.
  • Explore the Queen's Bath, a luxurious bathing complex with intricate water channels and lotus-shaped fountains.

    • Beyond the ruins:

  • Hampi isn't just about the past. It's a thriving village with a vibrant local community.
  • Wander through the Hampi Bazaar, bustling with colorful shops selling souvenirs, spices, and handicrafts
  • Take a coracle ride on the Tungabhadra River, soaking in the scenic beauty of the surrounding hills.
  • Climb the massive boulders that dot the landscape, offering panoramic views of the ruins.
